This study utilises three publicly available datasets for fire and smoke detection tasks. The D-Fire dataset is available at https://github.com/gaia-solutions-on-demand/DFireDataset and is detailed in the literature: De Venâncio, P. V. A., Rezende, T. M., Lisboa, A. C., & Barbosa, A. V. (2021, November). Fire detection based on a two-dimensional convolutional neural network and temporal analysis. In 2021 IEEE Latin American Conference on Computational Intelligence (LA-CCI) (pp.1–6). IEEE. The FLAME dataset can be accessed at https://github.com/AlirezaShamsoshoara/Fire-Detection-UAV-Aerial-Image-Classification-Segmentation-UnmannedAerialVehicle and is detailed in the literature: Shamsoshoara, A., Afghah, F., Razi, A., Zheng, L., Fulé, P. Z., & Blasch, E. (2021). Aerial imagery pile burn detection using deep learning: The FLAME dataset. Computer Networks, 193, 108001. The M4SFWD dataset is available at https://github.com/Philharmy-Wang/M4SFWD and is detailed in the literature: Wang, G., Li, H., Li, P., Lang, X., Feng, Y., Ding, Z., & Xie, S. (2024). M4SFWD: A Multi-Faceted synthetic dataset for remote sensing forest wildfires detection. Expert Systems with Applications, 248, 123489.
